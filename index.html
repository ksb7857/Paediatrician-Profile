Specialist Paediatrician

North Shore Private Hospital

Dr Ketaki Sharma (Ket) is an experienced paediatrician who recognises the importance of listening to families and aims to deliver tailored, family-centred support.

Ket's clinical interests include:

  • Newborn and infant health concerns;
  • Infant reflux;
  • Growth or weight concerns;
  • Suspected cow’s milk protein intolerance;
  • Food allergies (skin prick testing available on-site, if clinically required);
  • Eczema;
  • Hayfever;
  • Immunisation advice;
  • Constipation, and other common childhood concerns.

Professional Background

Ket is also a Staff Specialist Paediatrician at:

  • The Children’s Hospital at Westmead (Specialist Immunisation Service)
  • The National Centre for Immunisation Research and Surveillance (NCIRS)

Ket obtained her medical degree and a Master of Public Health at the University of New South Wales. She undertook specialist paediatric training through the Sydney Children’s Hospital Network and is a Fellow of the Royal Australasian College of Physicians (FRACP).

Her commitment to evidence-based practice and lifelong learning ensures she stays informed of changes in clinical guidelines. She is an Associate Member of the Australasian Society for Clinical Immunology and Allergy (ASCIA) and a member of the Australian Paediatric Society.

Beyond her professional training, Ket has two children, one of whom has multiple food allergies, eczema, and hayfever. This lived experience informs her empathetic, practical approach to supporting families facing similar challenges.

Ket is passionate about promoting the evidence-based use of vaccines in Australia and globally. Her work includes:

  • Developing national immunisation guidelines;
  • Contributing to research (view her research profile); and
  • Contributing to global health initiatives, including collaborations with the World Health Organization.

Teaching

Ket enjoys teaching and holds a Conjoint Appointment as Clinical Lecturer in the Discipline of Child and Adolescent Health at the University of Sydney. She also regularly delivers educational updates for GPs and other specialists.
